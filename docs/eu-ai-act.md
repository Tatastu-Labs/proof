# EU AI Act Article 50 Compliance

## The requirement

[EU AI Act Article 50(4)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024R1689)
requires providers of general-purpose AI systems to mark AI-generated content with
**machine-readable metadata** indicating that it was AI-generated. The obligation
applies from **2 August 2026**.

This applies broadly: text, image, audio, video, and other content generated by
AI systems must carry discernible, durable labels — including in cases where the
content is not immediately obvious as AI-generated (summaries, translations, code
with AI assistance, etc.).

## What Tatastu Proof provides

A Tatastu Proof stamp does three things that matter for Article 50 compliance:

1. **Machine-readable provenance metadata** — every stamp returns a `_proof` block
   you can attach to any output. It contains the SHA-256 of the content, a canonical
   proof ID, a verify URL, and a byline ready for embedding.

2. **Tamper-evident anchoring** — within 24 hours, the stamp is included in a
   Merkle tree anchored to Arweave and Base. The hash, creator identity, and
   signing time cannot be altered after anchoring without invalidating the proof.

3. **Permanent public verifiability**: the verify URL at `proof.tatastu.dev/p/{id}`
   is always resolvable. Anyone can confirm the stamp without an account, API key, or
   our cooperation (via the offline verifier).

## Honest framing

Article 50 requires **disclosure**, not a guarantee of origin truth. A Tatastu stamp
records that a specific signer claimed specific content at a specific time. It does
not assert that the content was AI-generated on its own — your system must apply the
stamp to AI-generated outputs and document that policy.

- **Claimed time** (`signedAt`): when the stamp was signed by the service.
- **Proven time** (`ANCHORED`): when the Merkle root was written to Arweave/Base.
- The stamp proves the former immediately; the latter follows within 24 hours.

## Revocation

If AI-generated content is later retracted or found to violate policy, call
`revoke({ proofId, apiKey })` — the verify page will show "Revoked" with a
timestamp. Revocation is non-destructive: the original stamp remains in the
transparency log; only the status field is updated.
